What Should I Do? – A Teen Guide

Helpful tips for tricky situations

1. Cyberbullying

If someone is being mean to you online:

  • Don’t respond aggressively.
  • Block and report the person on the platform.
  • Save screenshots as evidence.
  • Tell a trusted adult or school counselor.

Law: Many countries have laws against online harassment and bullying. In the US, cyberbullying can fall under harassment or stalking laws.

2. Being Sold a Faulty Product

If you buy something that doesn’t work:

  • Check the receipt or warranty.
  • Return it to the store or contact the seller.
  • Keep all communication and proof of purchase.

Law: Consumer protection laws often require sellers to provide refunds, repairs, or replacements for faulty products.

3. Witnessing Vandalism

If you see someone damaging property:

  • Do not intervene physically—stay safe.
  • Report it to local authorities or school staff.
  • Give a clear description of what you saw.

Law: Vandalism is illegal. Reporting it can help prevent further damage and legal trouble for those involved.

4. Finding Lost Property

If you find something someone lost:

  • Turn it in to the lost and found or local authorities.
  • Do not take it for yourself.
  • Try to return it to the owner if you can safely identify them.

Law: Keeping lost property without trying to return it can be considered theft in many places.

Stay safe, be responsible, and remember—there’s always someone to talk to!
